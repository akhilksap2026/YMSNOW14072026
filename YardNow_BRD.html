Executive Summary
Business context, objectives, and scope

YardNow is a production-grade Yard Management System (YMS) built for distribution centres, warehouses, and logistics hubs that need real-time control over inbound and outbound trailer movements. It replaces paper-based gate logs, whiteboards, and spreadsheets with a unified digital platform covering every stage of a trailer's lifecycle — from scheduled appointment through gate check-in, yard storage, dock assignment, unloading/loading, and final gate check-out.

The platform ships as a three-tier product (Core / Elevate / Enhanced) so customers can start with essential yard operations and progressively unlock AI-powered automation without re-implementing their workflows.

Business Objectives

  • Reduce trailer dwell time by providing real-time slot and dock visibility
  • Eliminate manual gate paperwork with structured digital check-in and printed gate passes
  • Improve carrier compliance and on-time rates through appointment scheduling and late-arrival tracking
  • Prevent costly demurrage charges with detention tracking and billing events
  • Give management immediate operational visibility via a live dashboard and exportable reports
  • Enable AI-assisted dispatch, email parsing, and predictive alerts for mature deployments

Scope

Product Overview & Modes
Three-tier offering — Core, Elevate, Enhanced

YardNow ships as a single codebase with three selectable product modes stored in localStorage (key: ymsnow_product_mode). The active mode is displayed in the top navigation bar and can be switched at any time by an Admin. Feature flags gate AI-only capabilities so that Core customers never see unactivated surfaces.

Mode Description Key Differentiators Target Customer
Core Full manual yard operations. Every workflow is explicit and human-driven. No AI involvement in any decision. Gate ops, yard map, dock kanban, appointments, inspections, reports, audit log Small–mid DCs, compliance-sensitive facilities
Elevate Core operations plus AI copilot assist surfaces embedded in the key screens. Dashboard AI brief, dock/appointment assist panels, smart suggestions inline, AI chat Growing DCs wanting AI guidance without full automation
Enhanced Full AI orchestration layer — proactive alerts, email intelligence, predictive congestion, and revenue intelligence. All Elevate features + email parsing, ROI panel, automated exception creation, lifecycle video Enterprise DCs, 3PLs, high-volume cross-dock facilities
Tablet Mode: All major pages support a 10-inch tablet layout activated by the Tablet button in the top bar or via URL parameter ?tablet=1. This optimises form grids, font sizes, and hit targets for handheld use at the gate or dock.

Dashboard
Route: /  |  Roles: All authenticated
Fig 6.1 — Operational Dashboard: live KPIs, action-required panel, revenue intelligence snippet, and product-mode banner.

Purpose

The Dashboard is the home screen for all authenticated users. It provides an at-a-glance operational overview of the entire facility — current yard inventory count, dock utilisation %, active holds, aged trailers, overdue moves, and units ready for outbound departure. The content adapts based on the active product mode: Core shows manual metrics only; Elevate and Enhanced add an AI summary brief and ROI panel.

Key Components

  • Operational Overview KPIs: Yard Inventory count, Utilisation %, On Hold count, Aged Trailers (24h+), Overdue Moves, Ready Out
  • Revenue Intelligence Snippet: Collapse/expand section with today's revenue and pending billing events
  • Action Required Panel: Prioritised list of items needing immediate attention (holds, escalated moves, unresolved exceptions)
  • Product Mode Banner: Dismissable onboarding card describing the active tier's capabilities
  • AI Assist Summary (Elevate/Enhanced): GPT-generated operational brief with top recommendations

Appointments
Route: /appointments  |  Roles: Admin, Yard Manager
Fig 7.1 — Appointments: weekly calendar view with colour-coded movement types and slot-conflict detection.

Purpose

The Appointments module manages the full schedule of expected trailer arrivals and departures. Carriers and yard managers can create, edit, and cancel appointments. The system auto-detects slot conflicts and surfaces late or no-show appointments for exception handling.

Key Features

  • Views: Calendar (Day / Week / Month) and List view with status filter tabs (All, Today, Late, No-Show, Confirmed, Cancelled)
  • Movement Types: Inbound, Outbound, Empty Drop, Loaded Arrival, Live Load, Live Unload — each colour-coded on the calendar
  • Slot Conflict Detection: Yellow dot indicator when two appointments share the same dock/slot window
  • Status KPIs: Today, Late, No Show, Confirmed, Cancelled counts updated in real time
  • Create Appointment: Form for reference #, carrier, trailer #, driver, time window, movement type

Gate Check-In — 5-Step Process

1
Find Visit
Search by appointment #, trailer #, or driver name. Select from results or initiate an Unscheduled Arrival (Walk-In).
2
Verify Identity
Three sections: Driver Identity (name + CDL last 4), Carrier Credentials (USDOT + SCAC), Vehicle (truck # + trailer # with mismatch detection). Discrepancies auto-create exceptions.
3
Load Details
Five sections: Seal & Movement type, Special Conditions (Hazmat toggle + Reefer toggle with °F input), Trailer Condition (Good/Damaged/Needs Inspection), Documents (BOL PDF/JPG/PNG upload), and optional Gate Notes.
4
Assign or Hold
After successful check-in, assign trailer to available yard slot or place a hold (Documentation, Security, Damage, Seal Mismatch, Driver Issue, Customs).
✓
Gate Pass
Print official gate pass with visit number, carrier, driver, trailer/truck numbers, movement type, check-in time, and any supplemental data (USDOT, SCAC, reefer temp, condition, BOL). Guards and driver sign.

Gate Check-Out — 4-Step Process

1
Find Visit
Search by visit #, trailer #, or truck #. QR code scan available.
2
Verify Unit
Confirm trailer and truck numbers match the visit record. Check Ready Out status.
3
Check Clearance
System checks for active holds, pending inspections, and documentation status. Blocks exit if hold exists.
✓
Exit Pass
Mark visit as exited, generate exit pass, update yard inventory count. Triggers billing events for detention if applicable.

Yard Inventory

Live table of all trailers currently in the yard. Columns: Visit #, Trailer #, Carrier, Location (zone + slot/dock), Status, Time on Yard, Last Update, Next Step quick action. Status badge shows In Yard, At Dock, Unloading, Loading, On Hold, Ready Out. Aged trailers (24h+) flagged in red. Group-by-Status toggle collapses rows into visual buckets. Alert chips at top surface holds, aged counts, and trailers awaiting slot assignment.

Yard Map

Interactive SVG canvas showing the physical facility layout. Zones are colour-coded: Staging Area A (inbound), Staging Area B (outbound), Reefer Yard, Hazmat Isolation, Parking Lot C. Each slot shows trailer SCAC code when occupied. Left sidebar shows operational queue sorted by time-on-yard. Top bar filters by zone, movement type, and status. Filter, zoom in/out, and fullscreen controls. Legend panel describes colour scheme.

Dock Management

Kanban board with 10 dock doors across 5 columns (Available, At Dock, Loading, Unloading, Completed). Each card shows trailer #, carrier, movement type, and SLA timer coloured green (on time), amber (at risk 2h+), red (over SLA 4h+). Critical-breached cards display a red "SLA Breach — Escalation Required" banner. Drag-and-drop cards between columns to update door status. Schedule view alternative to Kanban. 40% utilisation KPI shown in header.

Task management console for yard jockeys. KPI strip shows Unassigned, Assigned, In Progress, High Priority, Overdue, and Completed Today counts. Tasks can be filtered by status tab and searched by trailer/carrier/jockey. Cards show move type, trailer, from/to location, priority level, and elapsed time. Jockey Workload section collapses/expands per-driver task lists. Managers can create moves manually via "+ Create Move" button.

Holds & Exceptions

Centralised exception management. Types include: Manual Modification, Late Arrival, Documentation Hold, Security Hold, Damage Hold, Seal Mismatch, Driver Issue, Customs Hold. Severities: Critical, High, Medium, Low. Each exception links to a visit and can be assigned to a staff member. Filter by status (Open/Active, Resolved), severity, and type. Resolve button sends PATCH to /api/exceptions/:id/resolve. Exception count displayed as red badge in sidebar.

Inspections

Structured asset inspection module supporting multiple inspection types: Gate Inbound, Damage Assessment, and Yard Spot Check. Each inspection has a reference number (INS-XXXXX), linked trailer, assigned inspector, status (Pending/In Progress/Completed), result (Pass/Fail), and timestamp. The detailed form provides a checklist of inspection items, photo attachment points mapped to trailer hotspot views (front, rear, driver side, passenger side, interior, roof), and optional exception linkage.

Yard Audit (Yard Check)

Physical inventory reconciliation tool. Operators walk the yard and match physical trailer positions against system records. Each inventory item can be marked Matched, Mismatched (wrong slot), Missing (in system but not found), or Extra (found but not in system). Scope filters narrow to specific zones or dock areas. "Mark All Matched" button for rapid clean-yard certification. Results update audit records and can trigger corrective move tasks for mismatches.

Gate Guard

US-GG-001
As a Gate Guard, I want to search for an incoming truck by appointment number, trailer number, or driver name, so that I can quickly identify their record without manually paging through a list.

As a Gate Guard, I want to flag a reefer trailer and record its set temperature, so that the reefer yard can prepare the correct temperature-controlled slot.

As a Gate Guard, I want to print a gate pass immediately after check-in, so that the driver has official authorisation to enter the facility.

As a Gate Guard, I want a simplified full-screen Guard Mode interface, so that I can process check-ins efficiently on a gate kiosk without navigating the full application UI.

As a Yard Manager, I want to export operations reports as CSV or PDF, so that I can share performance data with stakeholders outside the system.

Glossary
Key terms and abbreviations used throughout this document
TermDefinition
BOLBill of Lading — legal shipping document listing cargo contents, origin, and destination. Captured at gate check-in.
CDLCommercial Driver License — mandatory licence for operating large trucks. System captures last 4 digits only for privacy.
DC / Distribution CentreA warehouse facility that receives inbound trailers, processes goods, and dispatches outbound trailers.
DetentionA charge levied against a carrier when their trailer exceeds the free dwell-time allowance at a facility.
Dwell TimeTotal time a trailer spends at the facility from gate check-in to gate check-out.
ExceptionAny deviation from normal operations: late arrivals, discrepancies, holds, SLA breaches, or damage events.
Gate PassPrinted authorisation document issued to a driver after successful check-in. Contains visit number, trailer/truck details, and signature lines.
Guard ModeStripped-down full-screen UI mode for gate kiosk stations, optimised for fast processing and minimal distraction.
HoldA status placed on a trailer that prevents it from being moved or checked out until the hold is resolved (e.g. documentation, customs, security).
JockeyInformal name for a yard spotter — a driver who moves trailers within the yard using a yard truck (also called a hostler).
KPIKey Performance Indicator — a quantitative metric used to measure operational performance.
Live Load/UnloadA movement type where a truck stays connected to the trailer while it is being loaded or unloaded, rather than dropping the trailer in a slot.
Move TaskA work order assigned to a jockey to physically move a specific trailer from one location to another within the yard.
ReeferRefrigerated trailer. Set temperature is recorded at gate check-in and the trailer is assigned to the temperature-controlled Reefer Yard zone.
SCACStandard Carrier Alpha Code — a 2–4 letter identifier assigned to transportation companies by the National Motor Freight Traffic Association.
SLAService Level Agreement — a time-based commitment for dock operations. Breach thresholds: At Risk at 2h, Over SLA at 4h.
SlotA numbered parking position in the yard where trailers are staged when not at a dock door.
USDOTUnited States Department of Transportation number — a unique identifier assigned to commercial motor carriers by the FMCSA.
VisitA single gate-to-gate event representing one trailer's stay at the facility. Created at check-in, closed at check-out.
Walk-InAn unscheduled trailer arrival with no prior appointment in the system. Processed as a walk-in visit.
YMSYard Management System — software that manages and optimises trailer and vehicle movements within a logistics facility.
ZoneA named physical area of the yard with a specific function: Staging Area A (inbound), Staging Area B (outbound), Reefer Yard, Hazmat Isolation, Parking Lot C.
