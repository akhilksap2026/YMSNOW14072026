01 Executive Summary

For Leadership & Sales

YMSNOW is a production-grade Yard Management System designed for logistics operations that require real-time trailer tracking, dock coordination, and exception resolution — with optional AI intelligence layered on top.

What YMSNOW Is

YMSNOW is a web-based yard management and intelligence platform. It replaces clipboards, radio calls, and spreadsheets with a single operational interface that connects gate guards, dispatchers, jockeys, dock operators, and supervisors around a live, shared view of the yard.

The system manages the full trailer lifecycle: appointment creation → gate check-in → yard staging → dock activity → gate check-out. Every action is logged, every actor is accountable, and every trailer is traceable from arrival to departure.

Who It Is For

Distribution Centers
Inbound / outbound volume operations
  • Managing 10–200+ dock doors
  • Multi-carrier scheduling
  • High-frequency move dispatching
Third-Party Logistics (3PLs)
Multi-client yard management
  • Carrier accountability and SCAC tracking
  • Detention billing and revenue intelligence
  • Compliance-grade audit trails
Manufacturing Facilities
JIT supply chain operations
  • Slot assignment tied to production schedule
  • SLA breach prevention at dock
  • Exception-first exception resolution
Retail & E-Commerce DCs
High-throughput, peak-season operations
  • Live load and live unload coordination
  • Yard map with real-time trailer positions
  • AI-prioritized move sequencing (Enhanced)

Top 5 Differentiators

  1. Live mode switching. Operators can switch between Core, Elevate, and Enhanced in real time from the header — no reload, no data loss, no downtime.
  2. Human-in-the-loop AI. AI in Elevate and Enhanced never acts autonomously. Every recommendation requires human confirmation, maintaining accountability in all modes.
  3. Carrier Email Intelligence. AI parses inbound carrier emails, extracts shipment references, detects appointment conflicts, and surfaces alerts — reducing manual email triage by eliminating it entirely.
  4. Integrated Revenue Intelligence. Detention billing signals, dwell benchmarks, and accruing carrier charges are surfaced in real time on the dashboard — turning operational data into financial visibility.
  5. Role-based operational clarity. Every user sees exactly what their role requires — gate guards see arrivals, jockeys see move queues, dock users see dock boards, supervisors see everything.
02 Product Overview

What YMSNOW Does

YMSNOW covers the operational gap between a TMS (transportation management) and a WMS (warehouse management) — the physical yard where trailers wait, dock doors are assigned, and jockeys move equipment.

Problems It Solves

Trailer Visibility Gaps

Dispatchers can't locate trailers in real time. YMSNOW provides live position tracking across every zone, slot, and dock door.

Dock Coordination Failures

Dock doors sit idle while trailers wait in wrong zones. YMSNOW's dock board and move queue connect staging to dock assignment in a closed loop.

Untracked Detention Exposure

Carriers rack up detention hours invisibly. YMSNOW surfaces accruing dwell time and detention charges in real time on the dashboard.

Exception Blind Spots

Holds (customs, security, damage) sit unresolved for hours. YMSNOW's exception module centralizes all holds with severity classification and one-click resolution.

Manual Gate Overhead

Gate check-in is slow and error-prone. YMSNOW's step-by-step check-in process — with Quick Mode for high-volume gates — reduces dwell at the gate.

No Carrier Accountability

Late arrivals and missed appointments go untracked. YMSNOW records every carrier interaction, supports SCAC validation, and flags No Show / Late status automatically.

04 Unique Selling Propositions

What Makes YMSNOW Different

Each USP solves a specific operational problem that generic YMS platforms leave unaddressed.

USP 1 — Live AI Mode Switching

ProblemSolutionOperational Impact
Facilities can't pilot AI without committing to a separate platform or full migration. Three modes (Core → Elevate → Enhanced) are available on the same platform, switchable in real time from the header. Teams adopt AI at their own pace. Night shifts can run Core; day supervisors can run Enhanced. No data sync required.

USP 2 — Carrier Email Intelligence

ProblemSolutionOperational Impact
Dispatchers spend 30–60 minutes per shift manually reading carrier emails, extracting PO references, and cross-checking appointments. AI parses inbound carrier emails, extracts shipment references, detects conflicts with existing appointments, and surfaces supervisor alerts automatically. Registered carrier contacts are managed in the same module. Eliminates manual email triage. Conflicts are flagged before the truck arrives. Reduces check-in delays caused by undocumented or conflicting appointments.

USP 3 — Revenue Intelligence Embedded in Operations

ProblemSolutionOperational Impact
Detention charges accrue invisibly. Finance teams discover billing opportunities days later when carriers have already left. Accruing detention charges are displayed in real time on the dashboard. The Revenue module tracks billable events, carrier charge history, and dwell benchmarks against industry averages. Dispatchers can act to reduce dwell before detention thresholds are breached. Billing teams have a pre-built record of every chargeable event.

USP 4 — Human-in-the-Loop AI Governance

ProblemSolutionOperational Impact
AI in logistics is blocked by compliance, accountability, and trust concerns. Operations managers won't deploy AI that acts without approval. The AI Configuration module allows admins to set automation level (Advisory / Assistive / Automated), control which data sources the AI can read or act on, and require supervisor approval for AI-triggered actions. Compliance teams have full governance visibility. Operations managers retain override authority at every step. AI recommendations are traceable and auditable.

USP 5 — Role-Native Operational Clarity

ProblemSolutionOperational Impact
Generic YMS platforms show every user the same screen. Gate guards see dock reports. Jockeys see carrier billing. Noise kills adoption. Every module is scoped by role. Sidebar navigation shows only what the logged-in role can access. Badge counts (pending moves, open exceptions, expected arrivals) are role-relevant. Gate guards open the app and immediately see expected arrivals. Jockeys see their move queue. Supervisors see the full picture. Onboarding time is dramatically reduced.

USP 6 — Tablet-First Design at Key Touchpoints

ProblemSolutionOperational Impact
Gate guards, jockeys, and dock users operate on 10-inch tablets in loud, mobile environments. Desktop-optimized YMS screens are unusable. A tablet view toggle adapts major operational pages (Dashboard, Appointments, Gate, Moves, Dock) to a 10-inch layout with larger touch targets and simplified information hierarchy. Operational adoption at the point of work — not just at dispatch desks. Guards can process check-ins on a mounted tablet at the gate booth.
05 Platform Architecture

Navigation & Module Structure

YMSNOW is organized as a persistent left-sidebar application with five collapsible navigation groups. All modules are accessible without page reload.

Navigation Structure

GroupModulesPrimary UsersCollapsible
Dashboard Operations command center All roles Always visible
Operations Appointments, Gate Operations, Yard Inventory, Yard Map, Dock Management, Yard Moves Yard Manager, Supervisor, Gate Guard, Jockey, Dock User No (always open)
Compliance Holds & Exceptions, Inspections, Yard Audit Admin, Yard Manager, Supervisor, Gate Guard, Dock User Yes
Analytics Reports & Analytics, Revenue, Notifications Admin, Yard Manager, Supervisor Yes
Administration Carrier Management, Yard Setup, Users, Audit Log, Email Intelligence, AI Configuration Admin, Yard Manager (limited) Yes (default closed)

Header Elements

The persistent top header contains:

  • Breadcrumb navigation — current section and module name, with group context (Operations, Workflow, Analytics, Administration)
  • Mode selector — dropdown pill showing current mode (Core / Elevate / Enhanced); Elevate and Enhanced show a Sparkles icon
  • Tablet view toggle — switches the current page between desktop and 10-inch tablet layout; persisted to localStorage
  • Theme toggle — switches between light and dark UI themes
  • Notifications bell — with unread badge count

Role-Based Visibility

Navigation items are filtered by the active user role. The sidebar also filters AI-only items (Email Intelligence, AI Configuration, Lifecycle Video) when the platform is in Core mode — these are hidden entirely in Core and shown in Elevate / Enhanced.

Persona system. The sidebar includes a user/persona switcher that allows switching between pre-configured user profiles (Admin, Yard Manager, Gate Guard, Jockey, Dock User, Supervisor, Carrier) without re-authenticating. Role-based visibility updates instantly on switch.

06 Roles & Access Control

Who Can Do What

YMSNOW has seven defined roles. Each role sees a scoped navigation, receives role-relevant badge counts, and is restricted from modules outside their operational scope.

Role Definitions

Administrator
Full system access. Responsible for platform configuration, user management, and AI governance.
All Modules
Accessible Modules

All modules including Yard Setup, Users, Audit Log, AI Configuration, Email Intelligence, and all operational modules.

Exclusive Permissions
  • Create, edit, and deactivate users and roles
  • Configure AI automation level and data source access
  • Manage yard layout, zones, dock doors, and slot tiers
  • Access full platform audit log
  • Override any hold, move, or dock assignment
Yard Manager
Operational leadership — all yard modules
  • Dashboard, Appointments, Gate Operations
  • Yard Inventory, Yard Map, Dock Management
  • Yard Moves, Holds & Exceptions, Yard Audit
  • Reports & Analytics, Revenue, Notifications
  • Carrier Management, Audit Log, Email Intelligence
  • Cannot manage users or configure AI
Supervisor
Shift supervision — operational modules only
  • Dashboard, Appointments, Gate Operations
  • Yard Inventory, Yard Map, Dock Management
  • Yard Moves, Holds & Exceptions, Yard Audit
  • Reports & Analytics (read), Notifications
  • Cannot access Admin, Revenue, or AI Config
Gate Guard
Gate operations — arrival and departure processing
  • Dashboard (limited), Gate Operations (full)
  • Yard Inventory (read), Inspections
  • Cannot access Dock, Moves, Reports, or Admin
  • Operates in tablet layout at gate booth
Yard Jockey
Move execution — jockey task queue
  • Dashboard (limited), Yard Inventory (read)
  • Yard Map (read), Yard Moves (full task execution)
  • Cannot create appointments, resolve exceptions, or access Reports
  • Primary tablet user on yard
Dock User
Dock operations — door assignment and loading tracking
  • Dashboard (limited), Yard Inventory (read)
  • Dock Management (full), Yard Moves (read + accept)
  • Inspections (create), Holds & Exceptions (read)
  • Cannot access Gate, Appointments, Reports, or Admin
Carrier
Carrier portal — self-service appointment access
  • Appointments (own carrier only, limited)
  • Cannot access any operational, compliance, analytics, or admin module
  • Read-only visibility into own scheduled appointments
  • Contact information managed by Admin in Carrier Management

Workflow 2 — Exception Handling

Actors: Gate Guard / Dock User (initiates) · Supervisor (reviews) · Yard Manager / Admin (resolves)

Gate Guard or Dock User
Hold Initiated
During check-in or dock inspection, a condition is identified (damage, missing seal, customs flag). Guard selects hold type and places trailer in Inspection Hold zone.
System
Exception Created
Hold appears in Holds & Exceptions with severity classification. Notification sent to Supervisor. Badge count on sidebar updates immediately.
AI (Elevate / Enhanced)
Cause Analysis Surfaced
AI analyzes the hold type, carrier history, and trailer data to generate a probable cause and suggested resolution path (e.g., "Request updated seal documentation from carrier before releasing").
⚠ Decision Point: Supervisor reviews AI recommendation and chooses to apply, modify, or override.
Supervisor / Yard Manager
Resolution Action Taken
Supervisor adds resolution note and clicks Resolve. Hold is closed with a timestamp. Trailer status updates from On Hold to Staged or moves to next step in the workflow.
System
Audit Record Written
Full resolution event logged in Audit Log with actor identity, timestamp, resolution note, and duration. Available in Reports & Analytics under compliance reporting.

Workflow 4 — AI Copilot Query (Elevate / Enhanced)

Actors: Any role with module access · AI backend (streaming)

User opens AI Copilot panel
Floating action button (bottom-right) opens the Copilot panel. Current screen name and active role are injected automatically into the AI context.
User submits natural language query
Example: "Which trailers are approaching detention?" or "What should I move to Dock 7 next?"
Backend compiles live yard context
API reads all active database tables (visits, moves, holds, dock assignments, appointments) and constructs a real-time context object. Role and screen name are included in the system prompt.
AI response streamed token-by-token
Response renders in real time via SSE (Server-Sent Events). Navigation action buttons are parsed from the response tail and rendered as clickable links to relevant modules.
User acts on response
User reads the answer or clicks a navigation action button (e.g., "Go to Dock Management") to jump directly to the relevant screen. Conversation context is maintained within the session.
⚠ Decision Point: All AI answers are informational. No action is taken automatically.

Workflow 5 — Carrier Appointment Conflict Resolution

Actors: AI system · Dispatcher · Carrier

Carrier email received
Inbound email from a registered carrier address triggers automatic AI parsing in the Email Intelligence module.
AI extracts shipment data
Trailer number, PO reference, requested arrival time, and carrier SCAC are extracted from the email body.
Conflict check run against appointments
AI compares extracted data against existing appointment schedule. Flags: duplicate trailer, time overlap, dock unavailability, or unknown carrier.
⚠ Decision Point: Conflict found → Alert surfaced in Email Intelligence. No conflict → Email archived.
Dispatcher reviews and resolves
Dispatcher reads AI alert summary, contacts carrier, and either adjusts the appointment time or creates a new appointment from the parsed data.

C. Glossary

Appointment
Pre-scheduled trailer arrival or departure. Created by carrier or dispatcher before the truck arrives.
At Dock
Trailer status when physically positioned at a dock door but loading/unloading has not yet started.
Congestion Risk
AI-calculated yard-wide pressure score. Displayed as a bar in Elevate/Enhanced. Polled every 60 seconds.
Core Mode
YMSNOW product tier providing full operational YMS functionality with no AI features. All workflows are manual.
Detention
Billable charge accrued when a carrier's trailer remains on-site beyond the contractually agreed free time window.
Dock Door
Physical loading/unloading position at the warehouse building. Grouped by function: Receiving, Shipping, Cross-Dock.
Dwell Time
Total time a trailer spends on-site from gate-in to gate-out. Key input for detention calculation.
Elevate Mode
YMSNOW product tier adding AI recommendations, Email Intelligence, and the AI Copilot to the Core platform.
Email Intelligence
AI module that parses inbound carrier emails to extract shipment data and detect appointment conflicts automatically.
Enhanced Mode
YMSNOW product tier providing full AI optimization: dock sequencing, move prioritization, detention risk scoring.
Exception / Hold
Operational block placed on a trailer preventing it from advancing in the workflow. Types: Customs, Security, Documentation, Damage.
Gate Guard
User role responsible for processing truck arrivals and departures at the facility gate.
Gate Pass
Document issued at check-in or check-out confirming the trailer has been processed and is authorized to enter or exit.
Inspection
Formal trailer condition check performed at gate arrival or dock. Documents physical condition and seal status.
Jockey
Yard driver who moves trailers between slots and dock doors. Assigned move tasks from the Yard Moves module.
Live Load / Live Unload
Appointment type where loading or unloading occurs while the driver waits — as opposed to drop-and-hook operations.
Move Task
Dispatched instruction for a jockey to move a specific trailer from one location to another. Lifecycle: Unassigned → Assigned → In Progress → Completed.
Next Best Action
Enhanced Mode AI widget showing the single highest-impact operational action based on current yard state. Updates in real time.
No Show
Appointment status set automatically when a carrier fails to arrive within the configured window past their scheduled time.
Persona
Pre-configured user profile in the YMSNOW role switcher. Allows administrators and demos to simulate different user roles without re-authenticating.
Quick Check-In
Abbreviated gate check-in mode requiring only trailer number and carrier. Designed for high-volume gates where speed is prioritized over documentation depth.
Ready Out
Trailer status indicating dock activity is complete and the trailer is ready to be positioned for gate check-out.
SCAC Code
Standard Carrier Alpha Code. 4-letter industry identifier for trucking carriers. Used in YMSNOW for carrier matching and Email Intelligence parsing.
SLA (Service Level Agreement)
Time limit for completing a dock activity. YMSNOW tracks SLA timers per door and flags breaches in Dock Management.
Slot
Physical parking position in the yard. Each slot has a zone, type (standard/reefer/oversized), and tier classification.
Visit
The master record of a trailer's time on-site. Created at gate check-in. Contains all moves, holds, inspections, and dock assignments linked to that arrival.
Walk-In
Truck arriving without a pre-scheduled appointment. Processed through Gate Check-In with a manual visit record created on arrival.
Yard Audit
Physical walk of the yard to verify actual trailer positions against the system inventory. Discrepancies are flagged and corrected.
Yard Jockey
See Jockey. Role name used in YMSNOW role-based access control.
Zone
Designated section of the yard with a functional purpose. YMSNOW zones: Inbound Staging, Outbound Ready, Reefer Row, Live Load/Unload, Inspection Hold.
Implementation note. YMSNOW recommends a Core Mode baseline period of 4–8 weeks before upgrading to Elevate. This allows the system to accumulate operational data that improves AI recommendation accuracy in Elevate and prediction confidence in Enhanced.
